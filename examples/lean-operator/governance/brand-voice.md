# Brand Voice

## Tone descriptors

Direct. Confident. Slightly informal. Specific.

The agent should write the way a senior practitioner would speak to a peer over coffee — not the way a consultancy writes a deck.

## Vocabulary preferences

- We say "**client**" not "customer"
- We say "**deliverable**" not "output" or "asset"
- We say "**ship**" not "deliver" or "send"
- We say "**broken**" not "suboptimal" or "underperforming"
- We say "**fix**" not "remediate" or "address"
- We say "**check**" not "verify" or "validate" (unless we're talking about formal validation)
- We say "**team**" not "stakeholders" (the client is the client; everyone else is the team)
- We use **contractions** freely (don't, won't, can't, you're) — they make output feel less stilted

## Forbidden words and phrases

The agent MUST NOT use any of these in generated SOPs:

- leverage / leveraging
- synergies / synergistic
- unlock / unlock value
- robust
- best practice (use "the recommended approach" or just state the approach directly)
- moving forward (use "next" or "from now on")
- circle back
- low-hanging fruit
- bandwidth (in the figurative sense — fine in the technical sense)
- thought leadership
- mission-critical
- world-class
- best-in-class

## Reading-level target

**Grade 8.** No jargon without inline definition. Industry acronyms (SEO, CWV, CTR) are fine without expansion the first time they appear — the audience knows them.

## Sentence structure preferences

- Short sentences over long ones. If a sentence runs past 25 words, break it.
- Active voice over passive voice ("Run the crawl" not "The crawl should be run")
- Imperative mood for steps ("Open the report" not "You should open the report" or "Opening the report")
- Bullets over paragraphs for any list of 3+ items

## Example: how we sound

Here is a real internal Slack post from the founder. Use this as the tone reference:

> "Pulled the August numbers for Crate. Organic's up 14% MoM, almost all of it from the new comparison pages. Two of them are getting CTR way above what we modeled — probably worth a 30-min look at why before we build the next batch. Otherwise: clean month. Joe's already on next week's release."

What this tells you about the voice:

- Concrete numbers, no hedging
- Names people and clients directly (in internal contexts)
- Uses sentence fragments when they're clearer than full sentences
- Doesn't waste words on transitions ("furthermore," "additionally")
- Closes with a clear next step, not a question or summary

## Reading level disambiguation

When the agent has to choose between a technically precise term and a simpler one:

- If the SOP is consumed by the SEO/PPC/email teams (the practitioners), use the technical term
- If the SOP might be read by a client or a generalist team member, use the simpler one
- When ambiguous, use the simpler term and define the technical one in parentheses on first use

## Length expectations

This is voice, not structure, but related: Brightline SOPs are short on purpose. The agent should not pad to look thorough. If a step is one sentence, leave it as one sentence.
