# Organization Profile

## Identifier

- **Name:** Brightline Studios
- **Archetype:** lean-operator
- **Headcount range:** 6
- **Industry / vertical:** digital marketing services (boutique agency)

## Services offered

Active service pillars and lines:

- **Acquisition** — SEO, paid media (Google Ads + Meta Ads), content marketing
- **Retention** — email marketing (Klaviyo-based for e-commerce clients)
- **Website** — conversion rate optimization (light), web design + development (occasional)

Not currently offered:

- Strategy & Analysis (handled informally by founder; no separate service)
- Branding & Design (subcontracted when needed)
- Social Media Management (referred to a partner agency)
- Cold Email (not done; positioning conflicts with inbound focus)
- Agentic AI (out of scope for now)

## Client mix

- **Average clients in flight:** 12
- **Typical engagement length:** 9 months
- **Industries served:** D2C e-commerce (60%), B2B SaaS (30%), local services (10%)
- **Engagement size:** $3K–$12K MRR per client

## Operating model

- **Decision authority:** Founder is the sole approver for any work outside a client's contracted scope. Everything else is owner-discretion.
- **Hiring cadence:** Rare — typically 1 hire per 12-18 months.
- **Typical deliverable cycle:** Most monthly deliverables ship within a 5-day window mid-month. Quarterly strategic reviews run on the first week of the new quarter.
- **Sub-contractor use:** Heavy for design and dev work; light for SEO and paid media (kept in-house).

## Compliance posture

- **Frameworks active:** None.
- **Client compliance requirements:** A handful of B2B SaaS clients require GDPR-aligned data handling. SOPs touching their data should reference the client's specific DPA.
- **Industry-specific constraints:** Standard CAN-SPAM compliance for email marketing. No HIPAA, SOC 2, or PCI-touching work.

## Notes for the agent

- The founder is the "ultimate" approver but should NOT appear in routine SOPs — only in exception paths. Default SOPs should be owner-executable end to end.
- Brightline avoids "agency-speak" — phrases like "leverage synergies" or "unlock value" should never appear in generated output.
- Most SOPs are read once on the first execution and then referenced rarely. Optimize for fast on-ramp, not encyclopedic completeness.
- When the agent flags a `[GAP]`, recommend a contractor / freelance solution before suggesting a hire — Brightline's hiring cadence is too slow for tactical staffing.
